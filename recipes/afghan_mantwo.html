Afghan Mantwo

Description:

Mantwo is a traditional Afghan dish that is steamed and then fried. It is a dumpling filled with ground beef, onions, and spices. It is typically served with a tomato-based sauce and a garlic yogurt sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 package wonton wrappers
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried mint
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Steps:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, onion, coriander, cumin, turmeric, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Place a small amount of the beef mixture in the center of a wonton wrapper. Moisten the edges with water, fold over, and press to seal.
  3. In a large pot, bring water to a boil. Add the mantwo and steam for 20 minutes.
  4. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the steamed mantwo and fry until golden brown.
  5. In a small saucepan, combine the tomato paste and water. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
  6. In a small bowl, combine the yogurt, garlic, mint, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Serve the mantwo with the tomato sauce and yogurt sauce on the side.
